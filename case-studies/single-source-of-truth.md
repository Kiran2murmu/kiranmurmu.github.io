# Case Study: From Scattered Updates to a Single Source of Truth

## Overview

A project tracking platform had been introduced to improve visibility across engineering and business teams. Despite the investment, project updates continued to be maintained in individual Excel trackers, while business stakeholders relied on emails and direct calls for status updates.

The lack of a centralized workflow led to duplicated effort, inconsistent reporting, and delays in decision-making.

As the Project Lead, my objective was to improve project visibility, streamline communication, and encourage adoption of a unified tracking process.

---

## The Challenge

Although a centralized project management platform was available, existing habits and processes remained unchanged.

Key challenges included:

* Engineering teams maintained separate Excel trackers for individual projects.
* Business stakeholders frequently requested updates through emails and one-on-one calls.
* Project status varied across Excel sheets, email threads, and the centralized platform.
* Teams spent significant time responding to ad hoc status requests.
* Leadership lacked a single, reliable view of project progress.

The problem was not the availability of a tracking tool—it was the absence of a consistent process for maintaining and sharing project information.

---

## Objectives

* Establish a single source of truth for project status.
* Reduce time spent on manual status updates.
* Improve visibility for business stakeholders.
* Standardize project tracking across teams.
* Increase adoption of the centralized platform.

---

## Approach

### 1. Understand Existing Workflows

Met with engineering and business teams to understand why they preferred existing methods.

Key findings:

* Excel trackers were customized to individual team needs.
* Business teams required quick access to project status without waiting for responses.
* Team members viewed the centralized platform as an additional reporting task rather than their primary workspace.

---

### 2. Standardize Project Information

Defined a common set of project fields including:

* Project owner
* Current phase
* Milestones
* Risks and blockers
* Target completion date
* Dependencies

This ensured that all teams reported project progress consistently.

---

### 3. Simplify the Update Process

Designed a lightweight update cadence where project owners maintained a single project record.

Instead of updating multiple trackers, teams updated one centralized location that could be accessed by all stakeholders.

---

### 4. Define Communication Guidelines

Established clear communication expectations:

* Routine status requests would be addressed through the centralized platform.
* Weekly reviews would highlight key risks and escalations.
* Direct outreach would be reserved for urgent issues requiring immediate action.

This reduced duplicated communication while maintaining responsiveness.

---

### 5. Drive User Adoption

Focused on change management rather than tool enforcement.

Actions included:

* Team walkthrough sessions.
* Quick reference guides.
* Regular feedback collection.
* Continuous process improvements based on user input.

The emphasis was on demonstrating value rather than mandating compliance.

---

## Results

Over the following project cycles:

* Project information became more consistent across teams.
* Business stakeholders gained greater visibility into project progress.
* Manual status requests reduced as stakeholders adopted self-service tracking.
* Engineering teams spent less time responding to repetitive update requests.
* Leadership had a centralized view of project health, risks, and dependencies.

The initiative improved communication efficiency while creating a more transparent project delivery process.

---

## Key Project Management Skills Demonstrated

* Stakeholder management

---

## Lessons Learned

Technology alone does not solve communication challenges.

Successful implementation requires understanding user behaviour, simplifying existing processes, and creating a workflow that benefits all stakeholders.

By focusing on process standardization and stakeholder adoption rather than simply introducing a new tool, the organization established a sustainable and transparent project management framework.
